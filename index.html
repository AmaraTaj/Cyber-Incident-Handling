BlackByte Ransomware Incident Response

Advanced Simulation & Documentation for Cybersecurity Training

1. Overview

This report outlines a simulated BlackByte Ransomware attack and the corresponding incident response actions. The exercise replicates a targeted intrusion against a corporate environment, aimed at assessing detection, response, and recovery readiness.

BlackByte is a ransomware family known for double extortion tactics: encrypting victim data and threatening public release unless payment is made in cryptocurrency. The simulation aligns with the MITRE ATT&CK framework to ensure realistic adversary behavior modeling.

This drill focuses on:

  • Understanding initial infection vectors and malware execution flow.
  • Documenting key Indicators of Compromise (IOCs).
  • Practicing coordinated incident response processes.
  • Improving security posture through lessons learned.

3. Attack Simulation Steps

  • Initial Access: Phishing email exploiting a known CVE.
  • Execution: JavaScript loader retrieves ransomware binary.
  • Persistence: Registry run keys altered for auto-start.
  • Defense Evasion: Shadow copy deletion to prevent rollback.
  • Impact: AES encryption applied; files appended with .blackbyte.

4. Detection Methods

  • SIEM flagged mass file modifications.
  • EDR detected suspicious registry changes.
  • Network logs identified outbound traffic to known C2 IPs.
  • Integrity monitoring caught unauthorized encryption operations.

5. Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

  • File Extension: .blackbyte
  • Registry Key: HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run
  • Known C2 IP: 185.93.2.77
  • Domain: blackbyte-encrypt[.]com
  • Hashes:
    • Payload: a5b2f4f77d3b4a7afbbf2c0a621b12345fae88e82c3ac53a2b39d1f84d0dfc8b
    • Loader: d1e3c4f77d3b4a7afbbf2c0a621b56789aae44e92c4bc51a3a22c3d84d1aefab
  • Files: encryptor.exe, payload.js

6. Response Actions

Containment

  • Isolated infected endpoints via SOAR segmentation.
  • Disabled compromised accounts; reset all credentials.
  • Blocked malicious IPs/domains at firewall and DNS filter.

Eradication & Recovery

  • Deleted malicious files, registry edits, and scheduled tasks.
  • Rebuilt systems from clean golden images.
  • Restored verified backups with SHA-256 integrity checks.
  • Staggered reintroduction of systems under active monitoring.

Post-Incident Review

  • Root cause: lack of early detection on obfuscated JavaScript loader.
  • Policy updates: new SIEM rules for JS patterns and mass-write alerts.
  • Shared anonymized IOCs with CERT and law enforcement.
  • Conducted IR tabletop exercise for continuous improvement.

7. Executive Summary

This simulation provided a controlled yet realistic environment to assess the organization's preparedness against a modern ransomware threat. The exercise validated the IR team's ability to detect, contain, and recover from an attack within a short timeframe.

Key achievements include:

  • Reduced mean time to detect (MTTD) to under 15 minutes.
  • Demonstrated effective use of SIEM and EDR for rapid triage.
  • Validated backup restoration strategy without data loss.
  • Established enhanced detection rules for early-stage infection attempts.
